# Scaffolding vs. Training: The Core Finding

The most important finding in adult EF intervention research, stated plainly: **external scaffolding consistently beats internal training.**

Not marginally. Consistently. Across populations, settings, and outcome measures.

## What This Means

**External scaffolding** = systems and tools in the environment that do the EF work for you. Reminders, calendars, checklists, structured routines, accountability partners, visible clocks.

**Internal training** = trying to build EF capacity directly. Working memory drills, impulse control exercises, cognitive flexibility tasks.

The principle: *automate and externalize, not remediate.* Design your life to work within your actual EF capacity, not above it.

## Why Training Fails to Generalize

Cognitive training does produce real improvements — but only on tasks similar to what you practised (called *near-transfer*, effect size g≈0.44–0.46). What it doesn't do is improve unrelated EF tasks or real-world functioning (*far-transfer*, g≈0.11–0.12 — close to zero once you account for placebo effects and publication bias).

The reason: training improves a specific cognitive route, not the underlying capacity. Stop training, gains fade. Two independent meta-analyses confirm this:
- **Melby-Lervåg, Redick & Hulme (2016)** — 87 publications, 145 comparisons. Near-transfer reliable; far-transfer (fluid intelligence, verbal ability, reading, arithmetic) no convincing improvement vs. active control. [PMC4968033](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4968033/)
- **Sala & Gobet (2019)** — second-order meta-analysis. Near g=0.46, far g=0.12; far-transfer approaches zero after correcting for publication bias. [Collabra Psychology](https://online.ucpress.edu/collabra/article/5/1/18/113004/)

## Why Scaffolding Works

Scaffolding removes the moment-to-moment demand on EF. You don't need to remember — the reminder fires. You don't need to prioritize — the list is already ordered. You don't need to estimate time — the timer shows it.

Evidence: strong and replicable. Works in ADHD, traumatic brain injury, depression, aging. The underlying deficit doesn't matter much — the scaffold compensates regardless.

**Limitation:** Scaffolding persists only while scaffolds remain in place. It compensates; it doesn't build.

## Specific Techniques with Evidence

**Implementation intentions** — Pre-script if-then responses to predictable triggers. "If it's 9am Monday, I open the report before email." Automates the decision before the moment arrives. Removes the need for EF at the point of action. Evidence: moderate-to-strong.

**Reward proximity** — Move rewards closer to the behaviour. Don't wait for task completion. Reward the first 10 minutes. ADHD is associated with steeper delay discounting — future rewards are devalued more steeply than in neurotypical peers, a finding replicated across multiple meta-analyses (Marx et al., 2021 — PubMed 29747508; PMC5049699). This is neurobiological, not a metaphor. Evidence: moderate-to-strong.

**Visual timers and anchor events** — For time blindness. Externalize time entirely. A Time Timer on the desk makes time visible. Anchor events ("before my 2pm call") replace abstract duration estimates with concrete contextual cues. Evidence: moderate-to-strong.

**Hierarchical task decomposition** — Break into three tiers: goal → intermediate steps (completable in 1-3 sessions) → micro-steps (15-30 min). Write each tier. Works when combined with external tracking; fails when self-directed only.

**Structured problem-solving sequence** — Write the problem → brainstorm solutions → pros/cons → implementation plan with timeline → execute → evaluate. Moderate evidence, most effective when embedded in coaching.

## Clinical Integration

Current best practice: scaffolding first, selective capacity building second.

Don't try to build your way out of a deficit you can engineer around. Reserve training for situations where scaffolding is impractical, or for building confidence — which is a real outcome, even if it isn't a neurological one.
