# Organisational Skills Training (OST)

A structured approach that directly teaches planning, time management, and organisation as skills — not as personality traits. Distinct from scaffolding (which routes around deficits) and from CBT (which targets beliefs): OST teaches the mechanics of getting organised.

---

## What It Is

OST programmes teach specific systems:

- How to maintain a planner or task list
- How to manage materials (where things live, how to retrieve them)
- How to break projects into steps with deadlines
- How to track homework, obligations, and commitments

Contingency management is often paired with OST — small, immediate rewards for using the organisational systems — because the motivation to maintain a system is itself an EF demand.

---

## Evidence

**Children and adolescents:** Evidence is solid. Multiple studies show significant improvements in organisation of materials, homework management, and time management. Some evidence that organisational gains also reduce core ADHD symptoms (Langberg et al., 2008 — cited below).

**Adults:** Evidence exists but is thinner. The problems are real — procrastination and missed deadlines are the adult equivalent of homework management failures — but formal adult-specific RCTs are limited. Current consensus: OST principles work for adults, but need adult-appropriate delivery.

**Evidence tier note:** No large adult RCTs as of 2024. This is a 3 (review-level evidence) for children; closer to 4 for adults specifically.

---

## Relationship to Other Interventions

OST overlaps significantly with:

- **Solanto MCT Modules 1–2** — time management and organisation are the core of the MCT protocol. MCT is, in part, a structured adult OST programme with cognitive restructuring added. If Solanto is available, it covers this ground with better adult evidence.
- **Dawson/Guare coaching** — the 12-skill model explicitly includes planning, organisation, and time management. The coaching format delivers OST skills with external accountability.
- **Scaffolding** — OST teaches you to build and maintain scaffolds; scaffolding is the output of OST done well.

**Where OST fits:** When someone needs to learn the mechanics of organisation rather than just be given tools. A person who has never had a functioning system needs OST more than they need another app.

---

## Limitations

- Adult RCTs are sparse; most evidence is for children
- Formal OST programmes are hard to access — usually delivered by therapists or school counsellors, not widely available as self-directed adult resources
- Skills learned in OST may not generalise beyond the training context without ongoing scaffolding (same limitation as all skills training)
