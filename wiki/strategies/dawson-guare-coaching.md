# Dawson/Guare Coaching Model

## What It Is

A practitioner framework for executive skills coaching developed by Peg Dawson (EdD, neuropsychologist) and Richard Guare (PhD, neuropsychologist), both at the Center for Learning and Attention Disorders, Seacoast Mental Health Center, NH.

Not therapy. Not academic tutoring. Sports-coach model — external structure, short feedback cycles, skill targeting. Direct quote from the primary text (p.27): *"Academic coaches work sort of like sports coaches: They help you decide what you want to achieve and then meet with you on a regular basis to make sure you're on track to reach your goals."*

## Theoretical Foundation

Dawson and Guare built their framework explicitly on **Barkley's 1997 developmental model** (*ADHD and the Nature of Self-Control*). Their books open by walking through Barkley's model as the theoretical justification before introducing their own vocabulary. They are not proposing a competing theory — they are translating Barkley's neuropsychological domains into observable, coachable behaviours for practitioners.

Confirmed by the ESQ-R validation study (PMC8430784): D/G's skills are described as "higher-order executive functioning skills" facilitated by the core EF factors (Miyake's three) — surface behaviours, not neurological constructs. When the ESQ is factor-analysed, it recovers approximately five second-order factors, much closer to Barkley's structure than to the face-valid 12-skill list.

## The 12-Skill Framework

Dawson/Guare map EF into 12 discrete skills (practitioner translation of Barkley's 1997 domains):

1. Response Inhibition
2. Working Memory
3. Emotional Control
4. Sustained Attention
5. Task Initiation
6. Planning/Prioritization
7. Organization
8. Time Management
9. Flexibility
10. Goal-Directed Persistence
11. Metacognition
12. Stress Tolerance

Assessment first: identify weak skills via the Executive Skills Questionnaire (ESQ). Coaching targets the weakest skills specifically.

## Coaching Protocol

Each session follows the REAP loop (p.37, Ch. 3):

1. **R**eview — what happened since last session; did the plan work
2. **E**valuate — why it worked or didn't; what to adjust
3. **A**nticipate — what's coming up; obstacles to plan around
4. **P**lan — build a specific, concrete plan for the next period

Short cycles. Behavioral, not insight-oriented. Collaboration and shared decision-making are central — coach doesn't prescribe, coach and client build the plan together.

## Adult Application

Originally developed for children and students. Adult adaptation: *The Smart but Scattered Guide to Success* (Dawson & Guare, 2016) — self-directed version of the same protocol. Map your own profile, apply the tools without a formal coach.

## Evidence Level

**Tier 2 — Practitioner Framework. Primary text read. No RCTs.**

No randomized controlled trials exist validating this model for adults. The framework is widely adopted in clinical and educational practice but the evidence base is expert consensus and practitioner experience, not experimental data.

Contrast with Solanto MCT (Tier 1 — has RCT with 42% vs 12% response rate).

## Where It Fits in the Stack

Sits between self-directed scaffolding and Solanto MCT:

- **Self-directed scaffolding** — you build and maintain the structure yourself
- **Dawson/Guare coaching** — external coach provides the accountability loop and co-builds the plan with you
- **Solanto MCT** — structured 12-session program with cognitive restructuring for beliefs blocking execution

Use Dawson/Guare when self-directed scaffolding isn't sticking and you have access to a coach. If no coach available, the adult book (*Smart but Scattered Guide to Success*) approximates the protocol.
